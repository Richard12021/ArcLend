Avara Docs

Everything you need to know about Avara on Arc Testnet.

What is Avara?

Avara is a multi-asset lending protocol on Arc Testnet. Users can supply assets, borrow against collateral, repay debt, withdraw liquidity, and monitor risk through Health Factor and liquidation mechanics.

Supported Assets

Avara currently supports USDC, EURC, and cirBTC. Each asset has its own market, utilization, supply APY, borrow APR, collateral factor, liquidation threshold, and oracle price.

Supply

Supplying means depositing assets into Avara. Supplied assets can be used as collateral and may earn yield depending on market utilization.

Borrow

Borrowing lets users take liquidity from the pool against their supplied collateral. Borrow power depends on collateral value, asset risk parameters, and current debt.

Repay

Repaying reduces your debt and improves your Health Factor. If your Health Factor is close to 1.0, repaying helps lower liquidation risk.

Withdraw

Users can withdraw supplied assets if the pool has enough liquidity and the withdrawal does not make their position unsafe.

Interest Rates

Supply APY and Borrow APR are based on utilization. When more assets are borrowed, utilization rises, which can increase borrowing costs and supplier yield.

Health Factor

Health Factor measures account safety. If Health Factor is above 1.0, the account is healthy. If it falls below 1.0, the account becomes eligible for liquidation.

Liquidation

Liquidation protects the protocol when a borrower becomes undercollateralized. A liquidator repays part of the borrower’s debt and receives collateral as a bonus.

Markets

The Markets page shows live protocol data including total supplied, total borrowed, utilization, rates, and market details for each supported asset.

FAQ

Is Avara on mainnet?
No, it is currently built for Arc Testnet.

Does Avara require approvals?
Yes, but the app handles approvals automatically before Supply, Repay, and Liquidation when needed.

What happens if Health Factor drops below 1?
The position can be liquidated.
